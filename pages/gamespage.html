Aaron's Top 10 Video Games

Honorable Mentions

Smash Bros. Ultimate (Switch)

Description:

My first honorable mention is a game that's the best of it's class. I grew up playing smash and it's great to have a refined version of the game with close to 100 characters from so many different properties. This game is a love letter to nintnedo and had to make the list in one way or another. Link and DK main

Red Dead Redemption 2 (PS4)

Description:

This game took over my life for about a month. The story of RDR2 is rich and easily immerses the player into the world of the wild west. Although the game has a ton of replay value for the story alone, it's not on the top of my list and the multiplayer left much to be desired. This is a game I look back on and think of dearly.

Sly Cooper: Honor Among Thieves (PS2)

Description:

Growing up, I wanted to be just like Sly Cooper. I always gravitated toward the "Ethical Thief" type characters like Sly, Nathan Drake, and Indiana Jones. This game holds a special place in my mind, but is definetly a product of it's time by current standards.

#10) Baldur's Gate 3 (PC)

Description:

Starting off my list at 10, is my most played game in the last year. I label myself as a retired gamer, but the game forced me out of retirement. The turn-based style of combat similar to old flash game I use to play, combined with the dungeon-crawling 2.5 D elements of the likes of Diablo and the roleplaying potential of D&D, created a perfect synergy of just good gameplay and storytelling. This is another game that completely took over my life for the first 6 months of it's release. The multiplayer aspect is seamless and jumoing in the campaign with a party of 4 brought me back to a level of joy from gaming that hasn't been matched in the last 4 years or so.

Review:

#9) Call of Duty: Black Ops 2 (Xbox 360)

Description:

Highly regarded from Brock as the best COD of all time, Black Ops 2 has three strong game modes in the campaign, multiplayer, and zombies to create a perfect boots-on-the-ground shooter before jetpacks and special abilities would take over the franchise soon after. It's futuristic theme while staying true to FPS shooters of old made the perfect mix and is still a game I blow the dust of my 360 for and run a couple rounds of zombies on Town with my friends. The zombies in particular was at it's peak in this game in my opinion. The MSMC absolutely melted people.

Review:

#8) Halo: Reach (Xbox 360)

Description:

Picture this: It's 2010, you're 10 years old, it's the first weekend of summer vacation, you click that silver disc on your 360 and load up a game, soon after you hear the most beautiful song known to man composed completely of hums and only one drum. I know you know the song. This is how pretty much every weeekend went for me for those next two years. Halo: Reach is buy and large the complete Halo game. This may very well be because it was my introduction to the series but that is my truth. The story of Noble Six and the Spartan III is a story that doesn't get enough high regards. THe campaign was awesome and the multiplayer was polished. However, the true game was The Forge. The ability to create your own custom maps and game modes was truly ahead of it's time. I still rememeber me and my clan playing custom modes like "Cops and Robbers", "Hot Pursuit", "Griff-ball", and "Speed Halo". This game just had endless possibilites and was endless fun.

Review:

#7) GTA V (Xbox 360)

Description:

I damn near worked in GTA V part-time. The grip that this game had on me at 15 was insane. I wasn't huge on the story mode but I remember it being pretty good, story was not a highlight. But GTA Online has to be my most played game ever. The mode was packed with microtransactions, pay-to-win mechanics, and was such an insane grind. But that never stopped me from clocking in and running some missions or jobs to afford that new car that Rockstar had dropped that week. The reason that it's so low on my list is that this game was, if anything, a social experience. PLaying with my friends and meeting new people through the game made an experience for me that I can't compare to any game. Although, playing GTA Online was pretty much impossible to progress with the ssame pace and wasn't nearly as fun. So that aspect knocked it down a couple notches.

Review:

#6) Mortal Kombat X (PS4)

Description:

I was absolutely cracked at MKX. This is a game I didn't play with anyone, becasue any friend that I played would rage quit. This is the only game I was natioanlly ranked in and one of the few games I took seriously enough to study the metadata and remember the amount of frames certain moves take and hitboxes and all of the things that make fighting games so in-depth. I played with Kenshi and his floating sword; me and Kenshi would play ranked for hours to try and rise the leaderboards. Fighting games were never on my radar too much, but something about this game completely ingulfed me and I find myself booting it up from time to time to let off steam. I'm suprised the servers are still up and I have no problem finding matches.

Review:

#5) Skyrim (Xbox 360)

Description:

Coming into the top 5, I got Skyrim. Skyrim is the one and only game I waited outside of a GameStop for. If I had a number 11, Oblivion would have made the list. Bethesda is my favorite developer of all time, and while their quality has fell off of a cliff, I look back at their older titles as some of the best times I've had playing games. Skyrim is the perfect example of a fantasy game and is pretty in-depth even by today's standards. This game has infinite replay value and with mod support on PC, Skyrim can become a completely different game. The story was intriguing and gameplay was a refined version of the Bethesda formula. While we may never get the Elder Scrolls 6 that we deserve, Skyrim does everything I need and more from an RPG.

Review:

#4) Saints Row 2 (Xbox 360)

Description:

This is a personal pick, I don't care. This game has no business with the likes of the titles before it, but this game was just such a moment in time in my childhood. While GTA was being dragged through the mud for being too graphic and violent in the media in the early 2000s, by mom would never let me get GTA until around GTA IV when I was older. But my mom had no idea what Saints Row was. My mom and my aunt bought this game for me and cousin and we became the biggest Saints Row fans, probably ever. This game was a mix of a gangster drama and a Seth Rogan comedy. The story and gameplay was such a wild ride and my cousin and I rode that ride until the wheels fell off. Those years and time on this game I look back on and I think of him; I would do anything to relive those times.

Review:

#3) Star Wars Battlefront 2 (PS2)

Description:

This game is the best adaption of the Star Wars franchise into game format. I was a big Star Wars fan growing up and Battlefront 2 had everything I needed and wanted. From the space battles, to the king-of-the-hill mode on all of the planets from the movies, and to the hero assault mode pitting the Jedi and the Sith against each other, this game had it all for a Star Wars fan. I have the Classic Collection on PC and is another game I hop on from time to time to unwind. Great shooter and battle sim.

Review:

#2) Smash Bros.: Brawl (Wii)

Description:

Smash Bros. is a game that will just never get old to me. While I find myself playing Ultimate these days, I had to give the nod to Brawl as my intro to the series. This game didn't seem possible to me when I first heard about it. Brawl had a suprisingly good story mode and battling my friends brought endless fun. Making custom stages and messing with different items and game mods left me on this game for hours. For every reason I stated under Ultimate, this game was that and more for me. I think I played Meta Knight because he was completely unfair, then when we banned him I moved to Link and Falco. Love this franchise.

Review:

#1) Fallout 3 (Xbox 360)

Description:

Yes, I know. The 3 is not a typo. Fallout 3 is greater than New Vegas in just about every category. I will say NV may have a slight edge story wise, but Fallout 3 had the better map and was everything for me in terms of a RPG and shooter. This was the qunitessential "hermit game" that had me stuck on the Xbox for an entire week. I remember this was the first time I started toying with the 360's devkit in order to add mods to the game. I almost bricked my 360 more than a few times but it would've been a worthy sacrifice for me to add more content to the game I love so much. This game and Oblivion were my intro to Bethesda and Fallout 3 in particular holds a special place in my heart to this day. The post-apocalyptic setting is one that I love and I am so deep into the Fallout lore, more than any other property.
