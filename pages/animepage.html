Brock's Top 10 Animes

Honorable Mentions

Vinland Saga

Description:

Starting off the honorable mentions list is Vinland Saga. It is about young man named Thorfinn who finds himself trying to take revenge against a man who killed his father when he was a young lad. He grows up on the man's ship learning how to fight, pillage, and kill, all while trying to kill him. I love this anime for its ability to bring conflicting characters into a war and shows us that no one is ever truely good or evil on the battlefield. Thorfinn is also so focused on revenge, and fails to see that the man who kills his father is the only reason that he himself is still alive. If you love vikings, action, and character development. This anime is for you!

Hell's Paradise

Description:

Next on my honorable mentions list is a new anime to the scene in Hell's Paradise. It is only one season long but the first episode is one of the best pilot episodes I have ever watching. Here we follow Gabimaru, a ruthless killer who has been captured. Instead of execution, him and others like him are tasked with going upon a mysterious island that has killed all those who have set foot there. Guards are tasked with escorting these killers onto the island to find a specific treasure that the ruler wants. However, both parties soon realize that this island is far more dangerous than they realized. Supernatural stuff starts happening around them and it becomes a fight for survival where both parties need to work together. This anime exceeded my expectiations when watching it and I am looking forward to season two and maybe moving it into my top 10.

Arcane

Description:

My final honorable mention has to go to Arcane. Now if this was top 10 animated shows this might be 1 or 2 because it is so well written and the animation, characters, and soundtrack are the epitemy of perfection. However, this is technically not an anime so I could not put this on my top 10 list for that reason. Arcane is based off the game Leauge of Legends and follows the relationship between two sisters Vi and Powder. In the beginning of the show, a man named Vandar takes them in and raises them as his own. Everything starts to change when Vi and her group steal from the upper city of Piltover and cause an explosion, leading to an all out manhunt in the underground. This series follows the depths of betrayal, civil war, and how some bonds can make or break an individual's mind. This is a must watch!

#10) Hunter x Hunter

Description:

Starting off my top 10 is Hunter x Hunter. This anime follows the adventures of a kid named Gon who wants to became a pro hunter. He sets off to take the entrance exam where thousands test but only a select few are able to pass. On his adventure, he meets friends who help him on his journey as well as people who challenge him to get stronger even as a child. Hunter x Hunter is one of the highest rated animes of all time and rightly so as almost every single episode and arc has plenty of action, character development, and world building. The storyline is amazing and it continues to get better the farther into it you get. I love the diversity of characters and the power system in Hunter x Hunter and this anime contains on of my favorite all time anime characters in Killua. This is a longer, but very beginner friendly anime and was one of the first anime I ever watched when I used to think anime was dumb.

#9) Assassination Classroom

Description:

Assassination Classroom might be one of the best anime every created in terms of character building. This anime is about an alien named Koro-sensai who is threating to blow up the rest of the moon at the end of one year. He has only one request... to teach a bunch of highschoolers who have been cast away as nobodies and to make them try to kill him. Not only that, he wants to make them the best class by bringing out their full potential in their educaiton as well. If you think that sounds crazy and intriguing, well it sure is. Not only that this anime makes you fall in love with all sorts of colorful characters who most would think are irredimable. This anime will put you on a roller coaster of emotions and is only a couple seasons long. But man does it hit everything that it needs too.

#8) Spy x Family

Description:

Spy x Family is one of my favorite recent animes to come out and that is why is lands in the number 8 spot. Another storyline that peaks on interests as it follows a spy named Loid who needs to get access to a very private and influential man. The only problem, he has to get to him through his son who is only about six years old and in the most prestigous school in the country. Loid adopts a girl named Anya who is actually able to read minds, (He does not know this) and have her get close to the man's son. He then realizes he needs a wife to make his story credible and thats when he meets Yor, who we find out is an assassin (he also does not know this) and they become a fake husband and wife for their mutual benefit. This anime is a perfect slice of life, blended with action and hilarious moments the this very disfunctional and yet amazing fake family. This is one of my favorite feel good animes ever and am looking forward to more of the Forger family.

#7) Attack On Titan

Description:

Wait!!! "Attack On Titan only number 7... this is the most brilliant anime ever created..." Yes, I have heard it all and many people will think that I am crazy for putting it at number 7. Don't get me wrong, I aboslutely love this anime. The storyline, the plot twists, and the connection of everything, in my opinion will never be topped by anything for years, if ever. I think this is the most well-written anime of all time and when certain plot twists and reveals were shown... my jaw dropped literally. This anime will make you question everything about war, segregation, loyalty, and even will you side with a homicidal maniac out too destory the world. If people have told you anything bad about AoT, they probably don't understand the meaning or hated the ending, which I actually enjoyed. There are some characters that you wish death upon more than anything else in the world. However, this anime is a master piece and I think that more anime should follow the blueprints that this has set out for us... minus the 15 different parts it took to finish season 4.

#6) Frieren: Beyond Journey's End

Description:

Frieren is another anime with only one season to its name so far. It follows the adventure of a 1000 year old elven mage named Frieren after her and her part defeated the demon king decades ago. She finds herself not caring about life and only wanting to collect unique and bizarre spells as a hobby. She is then asked a favor by an old friend and ends up taking on an apprentice who follows her on her journeys. This anime is a bit slower paced compared to most fantasy type animes as it explores her backstory using various flashbacks as well as small little side adventures. She is very mellow and does not really care about time, but she starts to realize how precious it truely is for the humans she has encountered and become friends with. This anime is a perfect blend of action and character building as it explores the complex, albeit childish mind of an elven mage who all humans could never dream of becoming.

#5) Haikyuu

Description:

Now we get to the really good stuff in the top 5 and starting us off we have the famous volleyball anime Haikyuu. This anime follows a boy named Shoyo Hinata who wants nothing more than to became like his idol The Little Giant and be the ace of Karasuno's team. However, he has almost no volleyball game experience or technique that is required to play with the best. What he brings to the game is sheer speed, athleticism, and his insane jumping height. His new setter is a egotistic control freak named Kageyama, who wants to control every aspect of the game. He thinks Hinata is athletically gifted but refuses to set him until he can not be the weak link. These two build a bond over the seasons facing adversity from other teams as well as themselves as they try to become the best. This anime has some of the best sports animation I have seen and almost every single game will have you on the edge of your seat. The characters are all great and many of them are hilarious in their own way. My personal favorite is Bokuto. If you watch the anime, and I highly recommend it even if you are not a fan of volleyball, you will see why.

#4) Fairy Tail

Description:

This might be one of my most controversial ones as many people have been known too hate on it. However, at number 4, I have Fairy Tail. I understand some of the criticism behind it as the power of friendship and some fan service are definetly noticable. But that does not stop me from aboslutely loving this anime and the characters in it. I think this anime has done a great job at world building and showing us many different great arcs, but the thing I love about this anime the most is all the different characters in the Fairy Tail Guild. From the main characters Natsu and Lucy, to the wild Gray and Erza, to the absolute hilarous Juvia(my absolute favorite) and Gajeel, there are no shortage of amazing characters people can relate too. If you like darker animes, this one might not be for you, but Fairy Tail has some of the most memorable moments in anime and the slow build up of characters and their relationships is something that kept me going throughout the entirety of the anime.

#3) One Piece

Description:

One Piece at number 3 might have some people mad at me like my previous take on Attack on Titan. However, I currently have it at number 3 because I have only made it through about 800 episodes... yes there are still over 300 more for me to catch up on. For the longest time I stayed away from this anime because of its length, but man do I regret not watching it sooner. One Piece follows the adventures of a young pirate captain named Luffy who's life goal is to become king of the pirates and find the missing treasure called the One Piece. He forms a crew along his many different adventures and you start to really get a feel for each and every single member of his crew. The world building is absolutely amazing and each character gets their time to shine in the spotlight. This anime has made me laugh and cry the most out of any anime out there as it is just very silly, but nails the darker and more intense moments when it needs to. Think of One Piece like a marathon while most animes are a sprint or half marathon. It's alot more work but the adventure it takes you on is well worth the time. For me this has the chance to move up higher and even overtake my number 1 spot.

#2) Black Clover

Description:

Another shock for most people will be Black Clover sitting at number 2. Black Clover follows Asta who is a commoner born far away on the outskirts of the Clover kingdom. His dream is too become the Wizard King, who is the greatest magic user of them all. However, Asta was born with 0 magic, an anomoly in this world and hopes that maybe he is a late bloomer. Instead he trains intensely in his childhood years to build up his body and muscle to that 10x the average of a number person. However, he is still unable to use magic until one day a mysterious grimroire appears and gives him an anti-magic sword. This starts off his journey with his best friend/brother Yuno to see who will become the wizard king. Black Clover has some pretty detailed world building and really great animation and fights, but that is not what makes Black Clover special. Much like One Piece, you get to learn about every single character in detail and see them develop over many episodes. There are many classic and cheesy sterotypes, but the anime does a great job of giving each character their own unique personality and reaons for why they are the way they are. Don't even get me started on the amazing character development and storytelling of this anime. It does take a little while to get into kinda like One Piece, but once you get into it every arc gets better than the previous. If you love fantasy, magic, and amazing characters, I highly recommend you watch it... oh and its definetly got its darker elements to it.

#1) Eighty-Six

Description:

The moment everyone has been waiting for! What anime is my number 1? My answer might surprise you as Eighty-Six takes my number 1 spot. Many of you might not have even heard about this anime, many people have never watched it and it is one of the most underrated shows in the publics eye. However, every single thing that one might want can be found in a 24 episode anime. This mecha-anime story follows Vladilina Milize and Shinei Nouzen who both work together as handler and fighter. However, they have never met or seen one another and for good reason... the public believes that the drones that are fighting are unmanned. This lie has been going on for almost a decade, but there are real humans fighting and dying for them to live prosperous lives. Vladilina and Shinei are only linked through audio across hundreds of miles with Shinei being the commander pilot of one of the drone squads. This anime follows them and their "speadhead" squadron who are on the front lines waiting to die. This anime has it all -Politics, War Crimes, Action, World Building, character development, emotional moments of victory, , happiness, defeat, and death. Every episode is so important to the story and tells us so much. I can't say too much more as I don't want to spoil anything. If I had to recommend one anime that everyone should watch then Eighty-Six would be my answer. This anime is about as close to perfect as one could be and it caps it off with the two best finale episodes in any anime ever in my opinion.
